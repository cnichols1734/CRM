## Design Context

### Users

Primary users are real estate agents, transaction coordinators, and small brokerage staff who spend long stretches in the product during the workday. They are juggling listings, contacts, tasks, documents, and follow-up while moving quickly between desktop and phone.

### Use Cases

The product supports operational CRM work: managing contacts, transactions, follow-ups, documents, reporting, and AI-assisted workflow help. Most screens are work surfaces, not marketing moments. Speed, clarity, and trust matter more than novelty.

### Brand Personality

Calm, capable, grounded, and quietly premium. The interface should feel operational and polished, not flashy, playful, or trend-chasing. Think dependable real-estate back office with a little editorial restraint, not venture-backed AI demo ware.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use the current Origen CRM language as the baseline: dark slate shell, warm light surfaces, restrained borders, orange accent, minimal shadows, strong hierarchy, and practical density. Favor deliberate composition over decorative effects.

For product UI, keep the existing typography logic unless the user asks for a rebrand:

- Body copy and dense app surfaces should stay in the current system UI stack.
- Brand moments may continue to use the existing display choices already present in `templates/base.html`.
- Do not swap in generic AI-tool defaults like Inter, Roboto, or Geist just because they are convenient.

### Design Principles

1. Make work legible fast. The user should understand state, priority, and next action at a glance.
2. Preserve trust. Real-estate workflow screens should feel stable, orderly, and credible.
3. Use restraint. Let spacing, hierarchy, and contrast do the work before adding color or motion.
4. Build from shared primitives. Reuse the CRM macros, utility classes, and tokens before introducing new UI patterns.
5. Improve without drifting. New screens can feel sharper than old ones, but they should still look like the same product family.

### Anti-Patterns

- Generic purple, indigo, cyan, or neon gradient palettes
- Glassmorphism, glow-heavy dark mode, frosted overlays, or futuristic AI visual tropes
- Card grids nested inside larger cards, accent stripes on one side of rounded cards, or arbitrary dashboard chrome
- Oversized border radii, soft shadow soup, or pill buttons everywhere
- Centered-everything app layouts that waste horizontal space on desktop
- Empty states that feel like marketing illustrations instead of practical guidance
- Copy that sounds vague, inflated, or theatrically important
- Decorative motion such as bounce, elastic easing, or attention-seeking looping animation

### System Anchors

- Use `STYLEGUIDE.md` for the concrete design system and migration patterns.
- Reuse `crm-*` classes from `frontend/styles/app.css`.
- Reuse Jinja macros from `templates/components/ui.html`.
- Preserve the established shell, spacing rhythm, and interaction density on surrounding pages.
